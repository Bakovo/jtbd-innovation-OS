# Desired Outcome Engineering

## What is a Desired Outcome?

A desired outcome is a measurable statement of the result a user wants to achieve when executing a job step.

## Structure

[Direction] [Object] [Context] [Metric]

## Directions

- Increase: make something more/stronger/faster
- Decrease: make something less/slower/smaller
- Eliminate: remove something entirely
- Maintain: keep something steady
- Achieve: reach a specific state

## Quality Checklist

Good desired outcomes:
- Are measurable or observable
- Contain a metric or benchmark
- Specify the context
- Are solution-independent
- Use precise language (NOT vague: better, easier, more convenient)

## Example

Vague: Make shopping easier
Good: Minimize the time spent comparing product specifications across different websites when researching electronics purchases
