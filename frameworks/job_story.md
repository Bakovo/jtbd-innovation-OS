# Job Story Format

## Structure

When _____, I want to _____, So I can _____.

## Components

- **When**: The situation/context (not time of day, but circumstances)
- **I want to**: The motivation/behavior (what the user wants to do)
- **So I can**: The expected outcome (what progress means)

## Rules

- Never mention a product or solution in the job story
- The "I want to" should be about doing, not owning
- The "So I can" is about progress, not features

## Examples

Good: When I am hosting a dinner party, I want to cook impressive dishes without spending all day in the kitchen, so I can enjoy time with my guests.

Bad: When I am cooking, I want to use a slow cooker, so I can save time.
(Bad because it mentions the product - slow cooker - as the solution)
