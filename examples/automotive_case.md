# Automotive Purchase Case Study

## Context
20 in-depth interviews with recent car buyers (EV and ICE), China market

## Switch Analysis: ICE to EV

### Timeline
1. First Thought: Rising fuel costs, seeing EV on road
2. Passive: Reading EV reviews, noticing charging stations
3. Active: Test driving EVs, calculating total cost of ownership
4. Comparison: EV vs hybrid vs ICE, brand comparison
5. Decision: Range anxiety overcome by test drive experience
6. Experience: Surprise at smoothness, charging adjustment

### Four Forces
- Push (Strong): Fuel costs 4x higher, maintenance frequency
- Pull (Strong): Lower running costs, environmental identity, tech features
- Inertia (Moderate): Familiarity with gas stations, concern about resale
- Anxiety (Strong): Range anxiety, charging infrastructure reliability

## Key JTBDs

### JTBD 1: Daily commute without range concern
When: Planning my daily commute and weekly errands
I want to: Travel without worrying about running out of charge
So I can: Feel as free as I did with my gas car

### JTBD 2: Long trip without planning stress
When: Planning a weekend trip with family
I want to: Not have to carefully plan charging stops along the route
So I can: Focus on enjoying the trip instead of logistics

## Desired Outcomes
1. Minimize time spent planning charging stops on long-distance trips
2. Eliminate anxiety about battery range in cold weather
3. Maintain the refueling convenience level of gas stations

## Opportunity Scoring
1. Range confidence system (Core, 8.2)
2. Intelligent route planning (Core, 7.8)
3. Charging network transparency (Adjacent, 6.5)
